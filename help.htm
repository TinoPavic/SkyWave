Line of Sight (LOS) range is limited by terrain to 2-10km. Ground Wave (GW) range limited by terrain and ground to 10-60km. SkyWave uses reflecftion from ionosphere to cover entire Earth. NVIS covers from 0 to 600 km distance, 24x7.
Ionosphere changes all the time. As propagations change, optimal frequency will change. NVIS needs 3 frequencies: day, night and noon/extended. NVIS is effective from almost any site.
SkyWave covers much longer distances than NVIS. Most antenna sites are not very good for SkyWave. Obstacles and terrain often block low elevation angles.

Controls Explained

Lat - geografical latitude for Tx

Month –season of the year.

Year –sunspot cycle progression

SSN –smoothed sunspot number

Mast –antenna height

Antenna – antenna type (Rx & Tx)

P – transmit power in dBm

EL– minimum elevation angle for antenna site

d – ground distance between stations

hTx – Tx antenna height

hRx – Rx antenna height

Na - ambient noise at location (rural, suburban, city)

Ns – storms noise level at 2 MHz (ITU-R P.372)

BW – receiver bandwidth

Results Explained

foF2= three critical frequencies (night, day, noon)

MUF= three optimal frequencies to use (night, day, noon)

SSN= smoothed sunspot number (solar cycle)

M3k= Freuency multiplier for 3000 km distance (secant law)

B= Incidence angle into F2 layer (secant law)

f = frequency in MHz

Eirp = efective isotropic radiated power in dBm

Li = isotropic Free Space Path Loss

Ld = attenuation in D region ib dB (DRAP)

Lt = Li+Ld, total isotropic path loss in dB

N = noise power in dBm for given Rx bandwidth (ITU-R P.372)

SNR = signal to noise ratio in dB, higher is better

SnrM = SNR during mid-day (dB)

SnrD = SNR during day (dB)

SnrN= SNR during night (dB)

Fonts colours Black= night, Orange= day, Blue=Midday

Red fonts= frequency marginally high

Greyed fonts= frequency too high (no reflection)

Minimum useful SNR is 5 dB for data, and 10 dB for voice.
